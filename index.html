🌊 AI's Invisible Victim: Fresh Water

A single AI query — depending on model complexity and cloud region — can consume between 3.5 millilitres and 500 millilitres of fresh drinking water. Every time you ask ChatGPT something, water evaporates from cooling towers.
By 2027, AI data centres worldwide are projected to demand 4.2 to 6.6 billion cubic metres of water annually. To put that in perspective: that is roughly half of the United Kingdom's total yearly water usage.
Global data centres already consume approximately 560 billion litres of fresh water each year — and this number is rising exponentially with every new AI model deployed. Water scarcity is the hidden cost of your convenience.
Microsoft alone reported a 34% increase in water consumption from 2021 to 2022, driven almost entirely by AI infrastructure. In regions already facing drought — like parts of India, the American West, and Spain — data centres compete directly with local communities for access to potable water.

🌍 AI's HIDDEN CARBON FOOTPRINT

AI operations generated approximately 80 million tonnes of CO₂ emissions in 2025 — and this figure is rising at an alarming rate with every new large language model deployed.
A staggering 60% of all new data centre energy demand is currently being met by fossil fuels, not renewable energy sources. The "clean cloud" is a marketing myth — behind the interface, coal and gas plants are burning hotter than ever to power your AI assistants.
Global data centre electricity consumption is projected to double to approximately 1,000 terawatt-hours by 2030 — an amount that would eclipse the total electricity consumption of entire industrialised nations like Japan or Germany.
Training a single large AI model can emit as much CO₂ as five cars over their entire lifetimes — from manufacturing to scrapping. Every time you use cloud AI, you are contributing to a carbon footprint that is invisible but immense.
